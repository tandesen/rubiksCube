# Strategy

Updated: 2026-06-06

## Positioning

Cubic should sit between three categories:

1. Rubik's cube tutorial channels that teach solving algorithms.
2. Popular math channels that explain abstract ideas visually.
3. Serious algebra notes that prove theorems carefully.

The differentiator is not "teach people to solve cubes fastest." The stronger thesis is:

> The Rubik's cube is a physical model for understanding permutations, parity, group actions, invariants, and proof.

This allows two audiences:

- General viewers who want surprising math stories.
- Smaller serious viewers who want complete derivations.

## Business Model

Initial revenue hypothesis:

- Sell a learning bundle around the course.
- Proposed price: RMB 38.
- Proposed contents: 2x2 cube, 3x3 numbered cube, PDF notes, and course links.
- Candidate cost basis: ordinary 3x3 wholesale around RMB 6; 2x2, printing/marking, packaging, shipping, platform fees, and after-sales costs still need validation.

The product must be justified by learning value:

- Numbering should connect directly to the lessons.
- PDF notes should be useful enough that buying feels like getting a complete learning kit.
- The cube design should make cycle notation, permutations, parity, and piece tracking easier.

## Key Risks

1. The audience may like "interesting facts" but not buy cubes.
2. The math may become too rigorous too early and reduce reach.
3. The product margin may collapse after packaging, shipping, platform fees, failed deliveries, and customer service.
4. "Numbered cube" may need clearer visual/product design before it feels premium rather than handwritten or improvised.
5. The math claims must be exact. For example, God's number depends on the move metric, and public statements must be source-checked before publishing.

## Practical Validation

Before ordering inventory at scale:

- Publish 5-10 videos without product pressure.
- Test whether viewers ask where to buy the cube or notes.
- Make a small batch of hand-numbered or stickered prototypes.
- Sell a limited preorder or small batch before committing to a factory process.
- Track conversion by content topic: beginner solve, cube facts, group theory, proof deep dives, product demo.
